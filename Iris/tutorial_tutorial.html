How to create an IRIS tutorial

This will explain to you how to create a tutorial for IRIS. It's easy and fun!

Create your HTML page

An IRIS tutorial can live anywhere you want, it's just an HTML page which is well formatted in the following way:

<div id = 'tutorial'> <h1>This is the name of the tutorial</h1> <div> This is the summary of the tutorial and may contain any content/HTML you would like. </div> <!-- the next div tag contains all subsequent pages --> <div> <div> <h2>This is the name of the first step</h2> <div> This is the text of the step and may contain any content/HTML you would like. It does not have to be in a div tag. Anything after the h2 is considered content. </div> </div> <div> <h2>This is the name of the second step</h2> <div> This is the text of the step and may contain any content/HTML you would like. </div> </div> <div> <h2>This is the name of the third step</h2> <div> This is the text of the step and may contain any content/HTML you would like. </div> </div> <!-- and on and on as many times as necessary --> </div> </div>

BE CAREFUL - please make all links, images, and other references FULLY QUALIFIED URLS. Relative links will not work when embedded into IRIS.

Upload your content somewhere.

Your page may be placed anywhere you want. Note the URL.

Register in IRIS

Contact the appropriate person to register your tutorial into IRIS. Currently, that'd be Jim Thomason (thomason@cshl.edu)

Tell them the name to use when selecting the tutorial in IRIS, as well as the URL to pick up the content.

Sit back and relax.

You're done. Just kick back and wait for the changes to be deployed, with your new tutorial in place.
